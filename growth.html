Part 1

Baseline Snapshot

This visual shows the earlier report, where the pool candidate count was 132 out of 430 total candidates, which represented 30.7% of the full candidate base.

Analysis

In the earlier report, the recruitment pool represented a smaller portion of the total candidate population. The strongest concentration of pool candidates was between rank groups 100 to 300, with fewer candidates in the very top ranks and significantly fewer beyond rank 400.

This indicated that while the pool had a stable middle-rank base, there was still room to improve both the total size of the pool and its spread across stronger rank categories.

Part 2

Current Growth Snapshot

The latest report shows clear growth in both total candidates and pool candidates.

Analysis

The current report shows an increase from 430 to 517 total candidates, which is a 20.23% increase. More importantly, pool candidates increased from 132 to 214, which is a 62.12% increase.

This means the pool is not only growing in raw numbers, but also becoming a larger share of the total candidate base. Pool representation increased from 30.7% to 46.6%, showing that the recruitment pipeline is becoming stronger and more concentrated around viable candidates.

Part 3

Rank Distribution Analysis

The rank distribution gives a deeper view of candidate quality and spread across the pool.

Analysis

The latest pool distribution shows that the majority of candidates are spread across the Top 100 to 500 range, with the largest cluster appearing in 101–200. Compared with the earlier report, the distribution is now more balanced and broader rather than being concentrated mainly in the 100–300 range.

Another positive sign is the increase in candidates within the Top 100, which improves the overall strength of the pool. This wider spread suggests that the current recruitment effort is not just adding volume, but also improving the quality mix of candidates entering the pipeline.

Overall Growth Insight

The analysis shows that the recruitment pool is expanding both in size and representation. The sharp rise in pool candidates, combined with a wider and stronger rank distribution, indicates that the recruitment system is creating better pipeline quality over time.

Based on prior score analysis, candidates in the stronger rank ranges are likely to perform well in academy training. This makes the current growth pattern a positive signal not only for recruitment volume, but also for downstream readiness and performance.
